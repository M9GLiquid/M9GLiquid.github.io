School June 10, 2026

Master Thesis at Ericsson

My master's thesis at Ericsson is finished. The formal title is Grounding Text-Generated Human Motion into Executable Avatar Actions in Interactive Environments.

The project was about connecting text, scene data, and generated motion so a virtual avatar could perform actions in a Unity environment. It brought together AI reasoning, object understanding, and avatar animation in a prototype pipeline.

What made it interesting was the gap between a simple instruction and a believable action. A command like sitting down still depends on the object, the avatar position, the scene geometry, and whether the generated motion actually works once it is placed back into the environment.

Hobby April 25, 2026

Getting back into C with CCHAT

I have been away from C for a year or two, and I want to get back into it through something bigger than a small exercise. CCHAT is that project: a chat application in C where I can take one day at a time and build the system piece by piece.

The goal is not to rush it. It is a large project for me, and the point is to rebuild confidence with low-level structure, client-server thinking, build tooling, debugging, and the kind of careful implementation C requires.

AI is involved only for research, questions, and some bug-testing help. The code is 99% mine.

School December 31, 2024

Natural-language dungeon maps

I am experimenting with turning plain-English descriptions into structured 2D maps through the 2D Dungeon Map NL Generator. The project combines OpenAI-based feature extraction with rule-driven placement and A* pathfinding.

The interesting part is not only the map rendering. It is the pipeline: taking user language, extracting map intent, placing features, connecting them with pathfinding, and preparing the result so it can later be consumed by a game engine.

School June 2, 2024

Bachelor Thesis at Axis Communications AB

My bachelor's thesis at Axis Communications AB is finished. The focus was improving audio intelligibility in intercom devices, especially where wind or surrounding noise makes speech harder to understand.

The project was about audio processing, adaptive filtering, and practical embedded development. I implemented the solution in C on a Raspberry Pi setup that simulated an intercom, and used Matlab to evaluate different filtering approaches.

It was a good mix of theory and practical testing. The work started with comparing filtering methods, but the real challenge was turning that into something that could run on a small Linux-based setup and still be useful for noisy audio.

Work March 13, 2017

Launching Conquest on the KingConquest Network

Conquest is more than a Minecraft plugin now. It is launching as part of the KingConquest Network, which releases today, March 13, 2017. The launch video is still on YouTube: KingConquest Network launch. The server also had an official X/Twitter account: @KingConquesteu.

My role covers founder, manager, developer, and server manager. I am managing people as volunteer staff, including game staff, builders, and beta testers. Head admins handle moderators and admins day to day, but the head admins are still under my supervision.

Screenshots from that period include the old account header and some of the custom mob work. One part of the system was a mob spawner room with approximately 100+ custom mobs, ranging from shopkeeper NPCs to enemy mobs placed at specific locations. Examples included the Sky Bartender and CaveZombie.

More from KingConquest

Later, the network reached 100+ concurrent members, but eventually died. The combined role of founder, manager, developer, and server manager became too much, and recruitment efforts did not bring in the technical talent needed to keep it sustainable. Still, Conquest remains one of the clearest examples of early ownership in my portfolio.
